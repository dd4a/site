Accessibility outcomes and case studies

Digital Design 4 All partners with organizations to audit, remediate, and sustain digital accessibility. Below are examples of engagement structures and deliverables, anonymized for client privacy. Led by a CPWA-certified consultant with a PhD in Human Factors, our practice is grounded in standards development and enterprise experience.

Engagement types

Baseline audit

Outcome: Comprehensive assessment with prioritized roadmap—severity, affected users, and remediation paths across your product ecosystem.

WCAG audit services

Remediation sprint

Outcome: Ticket-ready fixes, code patterns, and QA protocols that your team ships in the next sprint or cycle.

Accessibility remediation support

Program buildout

Outcome: Definition of done, training, governance, and regression testing—accessibility embedded in your workflow.

Accessibility governance models

What we deliver

  • Findings reports: Severity, affected users, WCAG criterion, repro steps, and remediation guidance.
  • Implementation support: Code snippets, design patterns, and QA acceptance criteria your team can test against.
  • Executive summaries: Risk overview and business impact for stakeholders.
  • Scope: Web applications, websites, mobile apps, and documents (PDF, Word, etc.).

Selected anonymized case studies

Case A — Public sector web service

Context: Provincial government digital services portal with high public usage and procurement obligations.

Challenge: Inconsistent component accessibility, missing keyboard flows, and incomplete documentation causing procurement risk.

Role: Baseline audit, evidence package for procurement review, prioritized remediation roadmap.

Outcome: Reduced regulatory exposure, clear remediation plan implemented by the in-house team, validation report suitable for auditors.

Case B — Financial services mobile app

Context: Large financial institution with mobile-first customers and tight release cadence.

Challenge: Screen-reader inconsistencies, form validation issues, and accessibility regressions across releases.

Role: Remediation sprints, PR reviews, QA acceptance criteria, and automated checks.

Outcome: Faster remediation turnaround, fewer defects in subsequent releases, and reduced high-severity issues.
