Services

We work with organizations to audit, remediate, and sustain accessibility across web, mobile apps, and documents. Clear scoping, actionable deliverables, and partnership throughout. Based in Canada, we serve clients worldwide including the US.

Regulatory context & defensibility

We structure engagements to support regulatory compliance and defensible decision-making. Our work references applicable standards and laws, including:

  • ADA: Titles II and III considerations for public entities and places of public accommodation.
  • Section 508: US federal procurement and accessibility requirements.
  • ACA / AODA: Canadian provincial and federal accessibility frameworks.
  • EN 301 549: European accessibility standard for public procurement and accessibility claims.

Our audit artifacts, evidence packages, and validation reports are prepared to support legal and procurement review.

1) Accessibility audits

  • Scope: Web applications, websites, mobile apps, PDF and Office documents, and digital content workflows.
  • Standards: WCAG 2.2, EN 301 549, ADA/Section 508, and regional regulations as requested.
  • Deliverables:
    • Executive summary for leadership (risk, impact, recommended next steps).
    • Detailed technical findings with reproducible test steps, screenshots, assistive technology logs, and severity ratings.
    • Evidence package suitable for procurement or legal review (test scripts, timestamps, sampling notes).
    • Remediation roadmap with prioritization and estimated effort.
  • Optional: Assistive-technology testing, VPAT / conformance support, and regression QA plans.

2) Remediation support

  • Scope: Design-system updates, component-level fixes, front-end remediation, and document remediation workflows.
  • Deliverables:
    • Ticket-ready remediation tasks with acceptance criteria and code examples.
    • Patch reviews, pull-request guidance, and QA test plans.
    • Validation reports after remediation with re-test evidence and closure criteria.
  • Outcomes: Reduced exposure, repeatable QA, and clearer procurement-ready documentation.

3) Programs & governance

  • Strategy & governance: Accessibility roadmaps, definitions of done, role-based responsibilities, and risk registers.
  • Procurement & vendor management: Accessibility requirements, contract language, and evaluation checklists.
  • Training & enablement: Role-based workshops, reviewer playbooks, and accessibility champions program support.
  • Ongoing validation: Periodic compliance checks, continuous monitoring plans, and incident-response guidance.
Quick tip: If you need compliance, you usually need both: (1) a reliable baseline assessment, and (2) ongoing testing to catch regressions. We help with both.
