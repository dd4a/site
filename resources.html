Resources

Digital accessibility is built on standards, testing patterns, and team enablement. Below is a starter guide to standards we reference, practical checks we recommend, and tools that can help.

Standards & guidance

  • WCAG 2.2 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
  • WAI-ARIA Authoring Practices (APG)
  • EN 301 549 (ICT accessibility requirements)

Practical checks we recommend

  • Keyboard navigation: Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Space, Escape—can you use the app without a mouse?
  • Headings & landmarks: Logical structure that matches visual hierarchy.
  • Form accessibility: Proper labels, error messages, instructions, and status updates.
  • Color contrast: 4.5:1 for text; don't rely on color alone for meaning.
  • Motion & animation: Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for users sensitive to motion.
  • Mobile screen readers: VoiceOver (iOS), TalkBack (Android)—how does your app read?
  • Document structure: PDFs, Word docs—headings, lists, and alt text for images.

Practical WCAG interpretation

WCAG provides success criteria; our role is to interpret them for your product context so teams can make defensible choices. We document sampling decisions, test methods, and edge-case rationale so findings are auditable and repeatable.

Common audit mistakes

  • Poor sampling notes: Not documenting how pages/components were selected for testing.
  • Lack of assistive-technology evidence: Claims without screen-reader logs, keyboard traces, or screenshots reduce defensibility.
  • Ambiguous remediation guidance: Fixes without acceptance criteria or QA steps lead to regressions.
  • One-off fixes: Remediating a page without addressing systemic issues in patterns or CMS templates.

Procurement considerations
